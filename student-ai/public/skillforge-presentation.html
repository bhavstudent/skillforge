The Problem

Students are not job-ready
when they graduate

Colleges teach theory. Companies want practice. The gap between the two costs students their first job.

📚
Theory-heavy curriculum
College syllabus focuses on textbooks and exams, not on real-world coding skills or industry standards.
Gap #1
💼
No interview preparation
Students have no idea what Google, Amazon, or TCS actually ask in technical rounds until it's too late.
Gap #2
🤯
Expensive platforms
LeetCode Premium, Coding Ninjas, AlgoExpert — all cost money most students can't afford.
Gap #3
🚫
No personalized guidance
Generic tutorials don't know your weak spots or adapt to how you learn best as an individual.
Gap #4
